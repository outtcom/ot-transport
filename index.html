Last Mile & Freight FAQs

Everything you need to know about our last mile delivery and freight services.

Last mile delivery is the final leg of the supply chain — moving freight from a distribution centre or warehouse to the end destination. It's called "last mile" but can span hundreds of kilometres. It's also the hardest part: tight windows, customer expectations, proof of delivery requirements, and the highest failure risk. OT Transport was built specifically to solve this problem for commercial clients. We handle the last mile so your supply chain doesn't break at the finish line.

OT Transport serves all of Ontario, with a specialty in last mile delivery across the GTA, Durham Region, and beyond — including Toronto, Scarborough, Pickering, Ajax, Whitby, Oshawa, Hamilton, Ottawa, London, Barrie, and surrounding areas. Province-wide routes are available.

We haul commercial freight using box trucks: palletized goods, loose cargo, retail stock, construction materials, and general B2B freight. We are built exclusively for commercial operations — not household moves or hazardous materials.

Yes. Same-day and next-day delivery within the GTA metro area is available subject to truck availability. For urgent shipments, contact our dispatch line — we operate 24/7 for emergency freight requests.

LTL (less-than-truckload) means you only pay for the space your cargo occupies in the truck. It's ideal for businesses that don't ship full truckloads. We quote based on weight, volume, and distance — no surprise fees.

Absolutely. Scheduled recurring routes and dedicated fleet contracts are core to what we do. These include fixed pricing, consistent drivers, and priority dispatch. No long-term lock-in required — we earn your business each month.

LTL clients pay only for the truck space they use, quoted by weight, volume, and distance. One-off and same-day deliveries are priced per job.
